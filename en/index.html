Amazing Facts About Farting

You'll be surprised how much interesting stuff hides behind such a seemingly simple process.

01

10-20 Times Per Day

On average, a healthy person passes gas 10 to 20 times a day. This is a completely normal indicator of healthy gut microbiome.

02

3 Meters Per Second

The speed of gas exiting during a fart can reach 3 m/s (about 11 km/h). Comparable to a light breeze or brisk walking pace.

03

1.5 Liters of Gas

A person produces about 1.5 liters of intestinal gas daily. If collected in a balloon, it would have a diameter of about 14 centimeters.

04

Methane & Hydrogen

Only about 30% of people produce significant amounts of methane. Some people's gas can actually ignite — this has been experimentally proven.

05

Smell in 1 Second

Hydrogen sulfide, which gives farts their characteristic smell, can be detected by the nose at concentrations as low as 0.0005 ppm. The human nose is incredibly sensitive.

06

Termites Are Champions

Termites are record holders for methane production. One termite colony releases more methane than 1,000 humans. Nature is amazing!

Science

The Chemistry & Physics of Farting

Behind every fart lies a whole science. Let's break down what it's made of and how it works.

Gas Composition. A fart consists mainly of harmless gases: nitrogen (59%), hydrogen (21%), carbon dioxide (9%), oxygen (4%), and methane (7%). The characteristic smell comes from hydrogen sulfide — only 0.001%, but the human sense of smell is incredibly sensitive.

Speed & Acoustics. The speed of gas exit depends on pressure in the rectum and degree of gluteal muscle compression. The tighter the squeeze, the higher the frequency and louder the sound. Anus shape also affects timbre — like a wind instrument.

Gas Origins. Gases are formed in three ways:

  • Air swallowing during eating and drinking
  • Chemical reactions in the stomach
  • Bacterial activity in the large intestine

Health. Holding in gas can cause bloating, spasms, and discomfort. Doctors recommend not suppressing farts — it's a natural body cleansing process.
